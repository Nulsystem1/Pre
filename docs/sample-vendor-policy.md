# Vendor Onboarding Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Department:** Finance & Procurement  
**Last Updated:** January 1, 2025

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements and procedures for onboarding new vendors into our procurement system. The goal is to ensure vendor reliability, compliance with regulations, and financial security while maintaining operational efficiency.

## 2. Low-Risk Vendor Auto-Approval

Vendors meeting ALL of the following criteria may be automatically approved without manual review:

### 2.1 Financial Thresholds
- Annual expected spend is **less than $10,000**
- Payment terms are **Net 30 or longer**
- No upfront payment required

### 2.2 Risk Assessment
- Risk level assessed as **"Low"** based on:
  - Industry classification (not high-risk sectors)
  - Geographic location (domestic vendors only)
  - Business structure (established corporations preferred)
- Vendor has been in business for **at least 2 years**
- No adverse media mentions or regulatory actions in past 12 months

### 2.3 Documentation
- Business registration documents provided and verified
- W-9 form completed (for US vendors) or W-8 equivalent (for international)
- Certificate of Insurance (COI) with minimum coverage:
  - General Liability: $1M per occurrence
  - Workers Compensation: As required by state law

### 2.4 Verification
- Email domain matches registered business name
- Business phone number verified
- Physical business address confirmed (not PO Box)

**Action:** Vendors meeting all criteria above shall be **automatically approved** and added to the approved vendor list within 24 hours.

## 3. Medium-Risk Vendor Review Required

Vendors meeting any of the following criteria require manual review by the Procurement team:

### 3.1 Financial Triggers
- Annual expected spend between **$10,000 and $100,000**
- Payment terms shorter than Net 30
- Requires upfront payment or deposit

### 3.2 Risk Factors
- Risk level assessed as **"Medium"**
- New business (less than 2 years in operation)
- International vendor (non-domestic)
- Industry classification requires additional due diligence

### 3.3 Documentation Gaps
- Missing or incomplete insurance certificates
- Unable to verify business registration in standard databases
- Discrepancy between provided information and public records

**Action:** Route to Procurement Manager for review within 2 business days. Procurement Manager may approve, request additional documentation, or escalate to Finance.

## 4. High-Risk Vendor Block and Escalation

Vendors meeting any of the following criteria shall be **automatically blocked** and escalated to the CFO:

### 4.1 Financial Red Flags
- Annual expected spend **exceeds $100,000**
- Request for payment outside standard terms
- Vendor has past due invoices or payment disputes

### 4.2 Compliance Issues
- Vendor appears on OFAC sanctions list
- Vendor located in embargoed country
- Identified as potential shell company or high-risk entity
- Adverse media indicating fraud, corruption, or regulatory violations

### 4.3 Verification Failures
- Unable to verify business existence
- Email domain does not match business name (e.g., gmail, yahoo)
- Phone number disconnected or invalid
- Address verification fails

**Action:** Immediately **BLOCK** onboarding. Create case for CFO review. Vendor cannot be added to approved list without CFO written approval.

## 5. Special Categories

### 5.1 Professional Services Vendors
Vendors providing professional services (legal, consulting, accounting, IT) require:
- Professional liability insurance (E&O): Minimum $2M
- Background checks for key personnel
- Signed NDA and MSA before engagement
- Reference checks (minimum 2 references)

### 5.2 Critical Infrastructure Vendors
Vendors with access to systems, facilities, or sensitive data require:
- SOC 2 Type II certification (or equivalent)
- Cybersecurity insurance: Minimum $5M
- Security questionnaire completion
- IT Security team approval

### 5.3 Government Contractors
Vendors working on government contracts must:
- Provide proof of SAM.gov registration
- DCAA-approved accounting system
- Compliance with FAR/DFARS regulations
- Security clearances as required by contract

## 6. Ongoing Monitoring

All approved vendors are subject to:
- Annual re-verification of insurance and business registration
- Quarterly sanctions screening
- Performance reviews every 6 months
- Immediate suspension if risk level changes to High

## 7. Exceptions

Any exceptions to this policy must be:
1. Documented in writing with business justification
2. Approved by CFO
3. Logged in compliance system
4. Reviewed quarterly by Audit Committee

## 8. Policy Violations

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
- Vendor removal from approved list
- Contract termination
- Employee disciplinary action for unauthorized vendor engagement
